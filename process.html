The Infinite Descent Deconstructing Digital Habituation

Unveiling and Visualizing the Architecture of Digital Addiction

01. Idea Concept

The Origin of the Thought

Why can't we put down our smartphones from the moment we wake up until right before we fall asleep? Looking at my own screen time data—averaging six hours a day—I realized this isn't merely a lack of personal willpower, but rather the result of a deliberately engineered structure. I decided to dissect the digital environment that keeps users tied to an "invisible leash."

02. Research

Psychology & Design

Just like Brian Wansink's "Bottomless Soup Bowl" experiment, humans will continue to consume even when full if there are no visual stopping cues. I applied this psychological principle to the web's "infinite scroll." Furthermore, I intensively researched the "variable rewards" system—which keeps us constantly anticipating dopamine hits like a slot machine—and "dark pattern" designs that intentionally deceive our cognition.

03. Outcome Development

Outcome Development

Outcome 1 is a website that simulates the "digital trap" of endless scrolling itself. Outcome 2 is a physical installation that prints out the time wasted inside that trap as an actual paper receipt. By converting volatile, on-screen time into tangible paper, I aimed to force users to physically confront their digital habits.

04. Visual Design

Familiar yet Unfamiliar UI

I designed the UI to feel intimately familiar, mimicking platforms we consume every day like Instagram or Pinterest. However, behind the scenes, it is secretly tracking and recording every click and scroll depth of the user. It fully supports both Light and Dark modes to ensure high visual polish and complete user immersion in any environment.

05. Web Development

Web Development: Code that Chases Data

Going beyond a simple static website, I built logic that monitors and collects user behavior in real-time. I combined infinite scrolling with the IntersectionObserver API, utilizing sessionStorage to ensure accumulated behavioral data is maintained across page transitions. Furthermore, I optimized performance to ensure smooth operation even on a Raspberry Pi touchscreen.

06. Receipt Design

Receipt Design: Billing Your Time

The receipt is the core medium of this project. Initially, I planned to exhibit an infinitely long receipt on a wall, but it evolved into an interactive system that instantly prints based on individual behavioral data. I conducted dozens of print tests to ensure the typography remained crisp on the narrow 58mm thermal paper, and designed it to evoke a chilling sense of alarm by using terms like "Life Expectancy Cost."

07. Hardware Integration

Hardware Integration: Digital at Your Fingertips

I connected a capacitive touchscreen to a Raspberry Pi to recreate the exact tactile experience of scrolling on a smartphone. By integrating this with a thermal printer, I designed it so that when the user presses the 'Print' button, actual paper is dispensed with a mechanical sound. This creates a powerful moment where the "evidence of addiction," previously trapped in a digital screen, bursts out into physical reality.
